# Security Frameworks & Standards

## NIST Standards

### NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

**Goal:** Manage organizational security culture.
1.  **Govern:** Establish strategy and policy.
2.  **Identify:** Asset and risk management.
3.  **Protect:** Safeguards (Training, Access Control).
4.  **Detect:** Monitoring events.
5.  **Respond:** Containment and analysis.
6.  **Recover:** Restoration of capabilities.

### NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)
**Goal:** A 7-step life cycle for federal systems.
*Steps:* Prepare $\rightarrow$ Categorize $\rightarrow$ Select $\rightarrow$ Implement $\rightarrow$ Assess $\rightarrow$ Authorize $\rightarrow$ Monitor.

## CISSP Security Domains
The 8 domains defined by the (ISC)² for comprehensive security professionals.


1.  **Security & Risk Management:** Compliance, Law, Business Continuity (e.g., HIPAA).
2.  **Asset Security:** Protection, retention, and destruction of data/hardware.
3.  **Security Architecture & Engineering:** Designing firewalls and secure systems.
4.  **Network Security:** Securing physical and wireless links (e.g., Wi-Fi policies).
5.  **Identity & Access Management (IAM):** Controlling access (e.g., SSO, Biometrics).
6.  **Security Assessment & Testing:** Audits, penetration testing.
7.  **Security Operations:** Investigations and incident response.
8.  **Software Development Security:** Secure coding and application safety.

## Compliance & Best Practices

### OWASP Principles
For Web Application Security:
* Minimize attack surface.
* Principle of Least Privilege.
* Defense in Depth.

### Regulatory Bodies
* **GDPR (EU):** Protects EU citizens' privacy. Breaches reported in 72h.
* **PCI DSS:** Standards for handling credit card information.
