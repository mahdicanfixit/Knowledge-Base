### 🧱 Course 1: Technical Support Fundamentals

_The building blocks of IT: History, hardware, and the art of troubleshooting._

#### 🕰️ The Evolution of Computing

- **The Journey:** From Babbage and Ada Lovelace (the first programmer) to vacuum tubes, transistors, and integrated circuits.
    
- **Moore's Law:** Understanding the trend of hardware becoming smaller, faster, and cheaper every 18–24 months.
    
- **Binary & Logic:** How computers "think" using **0s and 1s**, and how logic gates (**AND, OR, NOT**) process information at the CPU level.
    

#### 🖥️ Hardware: Under the Hood

- **The "Brain" (CPU):** Executing instructions and handling calculations.
    
- **Temporary Storage (RAM):** Volatile, high-speed memory that clears on shutdown.
    
- **The "Skeleton" (Motherboard):** The central hub connecting every component.
    
- **Long-term Storage:** **HDD** (Mechanical/Capacity) vs. **SSD** (Flash/Speed).
    
- **The "Heart" (PSU):** Providing stable power to the system.
    

#### 💿 Operating Systems & Software

- **The Boot Process:** From **BIOS/UEFI** (Hardware Check) → **Kernel** (The Core) → **Full OS Boot**.
    
- **Major Players:** Windows (**NTFS**), Linux (**ext4**), and macOS (**Unix-based**).
    
- **The Interface:** Navigating via **GUI** (Visual) or **CLI** (Terminal/Command Line).
    

#### 🛠️ Professional Troubleshooting (The 6 Steps)

This is the "Bread and Butter" of a Support Professional:

1. **Identify the Problem:** Ask questions, replicate the issue.
    
2. **Establish a Theory:** What’s the most likely cause?
    
3. **Test the Theory:** Narrow it down.
    
4. **Plan & Fix:** Implement the solution.
    
5. **Verify Functionality:** Make sure it actually works and didn't break something else.
    
6. **Document the Outcome:** Write it down so the next person (or future you) knows the fix.

### 📝 My Hardware Perspective

- **PC Building:** Since I've built my own systems, I find the hardware section the most intuitive. Knowing how a CPU and RAM physically interact makes it much easier to troubleshoot a "No POST" (Power On Self Test) issue.
    
- **Logic Gates:** It’s wild to think that complex games and software are just millions of tiny switches (Logic Gates) flipping on and off.
    
- **Empathy in Support:** I learned early on that when someone's computer breaks, they are usually frustrated. Staying calm and actually listening is just as important as knowing the technical fix.
